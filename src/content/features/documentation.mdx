faqs:
  - question: "What kind of load documentation can I manage in DataDocks?"
    answer: "Any file that should travel with an appointment — BOLs, rate confirmations, packing lists, inspection photos, seal records, temperature logs, customs paperwork. Documents attach to the appointment itself so they're visible to everyone who touches that load, from dispatch through final close-out."
  - question: "Can I build custom QC checklists for different load types?"
    answer: "Yes. Build separate checklists for inbound vs. outbound, refrigerated vs. dry, hazmat, specific customers, or any appointment type your team tracks. Each checklist defines the steps, photos, signatures, or data captures required to close the load properly — and DataDocks enforces them so no load closes with incomplete documentation."
  - question: "Are checklists printable and usable on the dock floor?"
    answer: "Yes. Generate print-ready checklists for teams that still run paper, or run them digitally from a phone or tablet. The DataDocks iOS and Android apps let inspectors capture photos straight into the appointment — no downloading, emailing, or uploading from a camera roll — so QC photos, damage shots, and seal verifications land on the load record the moment they're taken. Most facilities end up using a mix: paper where inspectors' hands are full, digital anywhere photos and signatures need to be captured live."
  - question: "Do the checklists connect back to the appointment record?"
    answer: "Yes. Every completed checklist, photo, and signature attaches to the appointment, so when you pull up that load six months later for a dispute or an audit, the documentation is already there. No scanning paper files, no chasing someone's email inbox."
  - question: "Can I export appointment data and documents for audits or billing?"
    answer: "Yes. Export clean, structured data (CSV, or pushed to your BI / WMS via API) along with the associated documents. Teams use this for monthly billing backup, carrier dispute resolution, customer claims, and compliance audits."
  - question: "Can I require documents before an appointment can be booked or closed?"
    answer: "Yes. Make specific documents mandatory at booking (BOL upload before confirmation) or at close-out (signed POD before the appointment is marked complete). Rules can differ by customer, carrier, or load type so you only require documents where they actually matter."
