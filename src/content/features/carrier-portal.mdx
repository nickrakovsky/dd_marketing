faqs:
  - question: "What is a carrier portal?"
    answer: "A carrier portal is a self-service web interface that lets approved carriers and customers book, manage, and confirm dock appointments without calling or emailing the warehouse. It gives carriers 24/7 access to open dock times while the facility keeps full control over scheduling rules, capacity, and which carriers can see what. DataDocks' Carrier Portal works on phone and desktop with no app install required."
  - question: "Do carriers and customers need a login to use the DataDocks portal?"
    answer: "Yes. Each carrier or customer is set up as an approved account and logs in to see only their own loads. A single carrier or customer account can have multiple users under it, and a user can be linked to more than one carrier — useful for brokers, 3PLs, and dispatchers managing bookings for several partners from one login. Role-based access keeps each account isolated from other carriers' appointments and your operational data."
  - question: "Can carriers book appointments that break our scheduling rules?"
    answer: "No. The portal enforces your rules at the source — capacity limits, door restrictions, allowed time windows, required documents, carrier-specific requirements — so a carrier can only book into a slot that's actually valid for them. Your team can override rules with a deliberate warning; carriers and external users cannot."
  - question: "How long does it take a carrier to book an appointment?"
    answer: "Under 10 seconds in most cases. Carriers see open doors, pick a time, and submit from phone or desktop in a single flow. Every action is time-stamped so both sides have a clean record of who booked what and when."
  - question: "What happens when an appointment changes?"
    answer: "The portal sends automatic notifications to the carrier and driver whenever an appointment is booked, moved, or cancelled — including reminders before arrival. This replaces the chain of back-and-forth calls and emails that coordinators typically spend hours on every week."
  - question: "Does the carrier portal integrate with our WMS or TMS?"
    answer: "Yes. DataDocks provides a REST API out of the box, webhooks for pushing status updates to your WMS, TMS, or visibility platform, and an optional X12 / EDIFACT bridge for legacy EDI feeds. SSO (OAuth 2.0 / SAML) is available. You can pilot on a single door or day of the week before rolling out facility-wide."
