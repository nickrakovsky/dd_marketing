faqs:
  - question: "What can I limit capacity by in DataDocks?"
    answer: "Appointments per hour, per shift, per day, per door, and per dock are the common ones. You can also cap by load weight, pallet count, appointment type, or custom volume metrics — whatever your team uses to measure 'how full is this slot.' Multiple limits can stack, so a door can cap both 'no more than 4 appointments per hour' and 'no more than 100,000 lbs per shift' at the same time."
  - question: "Can capacity limits change by day, shift, or season?"
    answer: "Yes. Limits adjust for daily volume, shift patterns, peak seasons, holidays, and maintenance windows. Set a higher cap on weekday afternoons when you're fully staffed, lower caps on holidays, zero capacity when a dock is down for maintenance — all without editing the rule every time."
  - question: "Does capacity apply to the yard as well as docks?"
    answer: "Yes. Set capacity on yard areas the same way you do on docks — total trailers on-site, trailers in a specific zone, or total live loads staged. This prevents the common problem of a full dock schedule that still bottlenecks because the yard has nowhere to stage incoming trailers."
  - question: "What happens when someone tries to book over a capacity limit?"
    answer: "Carriers and external customers booking through the portal simply can't see over-capacity slots as available — they're blocked from overbooking at the source. Internal users can book over capacity when truly needed (a priority load, a last-minute exception) but DataDocks warns them first so the override is deliberate. Either way, capacity is never silently exceeded."
  - question: "Can I limit capacity for a specific carrier or customer?"
    answer: "Yes. Build carrier- or customer-specific caps — for example, 'Carrier X can book no more than 2 appointments per day' or 'Customer Y's loads count against a separate weekly cap.' Useful for balancing volume across carriers and making sure no single customer can swamp your dock."
  - question: "How is this different from the Custom Rules feature?"
    answer: "Capacity Limits are a purpose-built subset of DataDocks' rule engine focused on volume and resource caps — the numbers your team tracks to keep the dock and yard from overflowing. Custom Rules handle the rest: who can book what, required fields, approval workflows, time-window restrictions. Most facilities use both together."
