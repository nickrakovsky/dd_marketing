faqs:
  - question: "What devices does DataDocks run on?"
    answer: "Anything with a browser — desktops, laptops, tablets, phones. Windows, Mac, iOS, Android. No installs, no IT rollout. If your team has a phone on the dock floor, they can be using DataDocks in under a minute."
  - question: "Is there a mobile app or is it browser-only?"
    answer: "Both. DataDocks runs in any browser and also has native iOS and Android apps for internal teams who want push notifications and a mobile-optimized interface on the dock floor. Carriers and external users typically just use the browser portal — no app install required for them."
  - question: "Can the team use DataDocks from the dock floor, not just from a desk?"
    answer: "That's the point. Dock coordinators, QC inspectors, and yard drivers check live schedules, transition appointments, and capture photos from their phones — without walking back to an office to use a PC. Managers see the same live data from wherever they are, which is usually not at a desk."
  - question: "Does DataDocks need to be installed on company computers?"
    answer: "No. DataDocks is fully cloud-based — nothing to install, patch, or maintain on your IT team's side. New users log in through a browser and they're running the same version your whole team is on. Updates and new features roll out automatically with no downtime, so the dashboard your coordinator sees on Monday is the same one your VP sees on Friday."
  - question: "How do I give access to a new team member, carrier, or customer?"
    answer: "Admins invite new users through the dashboard. Internal users get the role-based access you assign them (coordinator, manager, yard driver, etc.); carriers and customers get portal accounts that only show their own loads. Setup takes seconds per user."
  - question: "Is access secure across all these devices?"
    answer: "Yes. DataDocks uses encrypted connections, role-based permissions, and supports SSO (OAuth 2.0 / SAML) for enterprise teams that want to tie access to their identity provider. Each user only sees the data their role permits, whether they're logged in from a warehouse tablet or a coordinator's phone at home."
