faqs:
  - question: "What metrics does DataDocks report on?"
    answer: "Dwell time, turnaround time by carrier, appointment adherence (on-time vs. late vs. no-show), door utilization, gate-to-dock time, appointments per shift, capacity utilization, and performance by customer or product. Every data point the system collects during normal scheduling becomes a metric you can slice by facility, dock, carrier, customer, or time range."
  - question: "Can I see which carriers are causing detention or late arrivals?"
    answer: "Yes. Break down on-time performance, average dwell, and missed appointments by carrier so you know exactly which carriers are driving detention costs and which are pulling their weight. This gives you fact-based data for carrier scorecards, contract negotiations, and conversations about preferred carrier status."
  - question: "Can I compare performance between docks, shifts, or facilities?"
    answer: "Yes. Compare throughput, dwell, and adherence across docks, shifts, dates, or entire facilities. Multi-site operators use this to find the best-performing location, surface practices worth copying, and catch facilities that are slipping before the quarterly review."
  - question: "Can I export reports or pipe them into our BI tool?"
    answer: "Yes. Reports can be exported or pushed to your BI stack via the DataDocks API. Teams using Power BI, Tableau, Looker, or an internal data warehouse typically pull DataDocks metrics alongside WMS and TMS data for a unified operations view."
  - question: "How fresh is the data in the reports?"
    answer: "Real-time. Because reports pull directly from the live appointment data, you're not waiting overnight for a batch job to update yesterday's numbers. A trailer that just left the dock shows up in dwell-time metrics immediately."
  - question: "Who on my team should be looking at efficiency reports?"
    answer: "Dock coordinators use them to spot live bottlenecks (a door running 40% slower than usual). Operations managers use them weekly for carrier and shift comparisons. Directors and VPs use them monthly for facility-level benchmarking and carrier contract decisions. Role-based views let each user see the slice that matters to them without drowning in data."
