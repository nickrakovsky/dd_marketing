faqs:
  - question: "What does live editing actually mean in DataDocks?"
    answer: "Every change to an appointment, trailer, or schedule updates immediately for every user who can see it — no refreshing, no 'wait, I just emailed you that.' A coordinator moves an appointment, the gate guard, yard driver, shift lead, and carrier all see the new state within seconds. No stale spreadsheets sitting on someone's desktop from this morning."
  - question: "Can multiple people edit the schedule at the same time?"
    answer: "Yes. DataDocks handles concurrent edits from multiple users across multiple devices. Two coordinators working different shifts, a manager reviewing the week, and a gate guard checking in a truck can all be in the system at once without stepping on each other's changes."
  - question: "What happens if two people try to change the same appointment?"
    answer: "DataDocks handles conflicts at the save layer — whoever confirms the change first wins, and the second user sees the updated state rather than overwriting it. Every change is logged with a timestamp and the user who made it, so if there's a dispute about 'who moved that slot,' the audit trail settles it."
  - question: "Do carriers and customers see changes immediately too?"
    answer: "Yes. When an internal user reschedules or updates an appointment, the carrier and customer assigned to it see the new details in the portal in real time — and receive an automatic notification. This replaces the phone-and-email chase that eats 10–15 hours a week in most coordination teams."
  - question: "Can I see a history of changes to an appointment?"
    answer: "Yes. DataDocks keeps an audit log of every change: who booked, who moved it, who checked in the truck, who closed the load. Use this for internal accountability, carrier disputes, billing backup, and compliance audits. No more reconstructing 'what actually happened on that load' from email chains."
  - question: "Does live editing work on mobile?"
    answer: "Yes. Coordinators on the dock floor, yard drivers in the field, and managers away from their desks can edit schedules, update trailer status, and capture load details from phone or tablet. The same live-sync rules apply — a change made on a mobile device shows up on the warehouse big-screen dashboard instantly."
