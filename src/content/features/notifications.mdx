faqs:
  - question: "What notifications does DataDocks send?"
    answer: "DataDocks sends automatic notifications for every appointment event — new booking, reschedule, cancellation, and arrival reminders — to the people who need to know. Coordinators, drivers, carriers, and shift supervisors each get the alerts relevant to them, so no one is chasing down updates by phone or email."
  - question: "Who receives appointment notifications?"
    answer: "You control it. Notifications can go to internal users (coordinators, shift leads, managers), external carriers, individual drivers, and specific vendors or customers. Each user or group can be set up to only receive the events they care about, so nobody gets alert fatigue from appointments that aren't theirs."
  - question: "How are notifications sent — email, SMS, push?"
    answer: "DataDocks sends email notifications by default and push notifications through the mobile app for internal users. Carriers receive portal notifications and email alerts when appointments change."
  - question: "Can I build custom notifications for my own workflows?"
    answer: "Yes. You can create custom notifications triggered by anything on the appointment — customer, product, carrier, dock, appointment type, current workflow stage, or any custom field your team tracks. Build a rule like 'alert the QC team whenever a frozen load checks in at door 3,' 'notify the night-shift lead when any appointment for Customer X moves,' or 'ping the yard when a trailer has been in the 'staged' stage for more than 30 minutes.' Notification text is also fully customizable so alerts use your facility's language and include the exact load details your team needs."
  - question: "Will carriers get reminders before their appointment?"
    answer: "Yes. DataDocks sends automatic reminders to carriers and drivers ahead of scheduled appointments. This cuts down on no-shows and late arrivals — the kind of misses that wreck dock flow and trigger detention."
  - question: "Does this replace the calls and emails my coordinators send every day?"
    answer: "That's the point. Coordinators typically spend 10–15 hours a week on manual status updates — calling carriers, replying to 'what time am I docking?' emails, notifying the yard about changes. Automated notifications cover the vast majority of that traffic so coordinators can focus on exception handling and building better schedules."
