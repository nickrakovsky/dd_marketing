## Just Promoted to Warehouse Manager? Here Is What Changes

Getting promoted from supervisor to warehouse manager is exciting, but it is also a completely different job. The skills that got you promoted, hands-on problem solving, being the go-to person on the floor, knowing every detail of the operation, can actually work against you if you do not adjust your approach.

### Key Takeaways

- **Your job is now systems, not tasks.** As a supervisor, you fixed problems in real time. As a manager, your job is to build systems that prevent those problems from happening in the first place. That means investing time in process design, [dock scheduling workflows](/posts/what-is-dock-scheduling), and standard operating procedures that your team can follow without you standing over them.

- **Learn to lead through your supervisors.** The biggest trap new managers fall into is bypassing their supervisors and managing the floor directly. This undermines your supervisors' authority and stretches you too thin. Trust your supervisors, coach them, and let them own their areas.

- **Get comfortable with data and reporting.** Upper management expects you to communicate in KPIs, cost savings, and throughput numbers, not stories about what happened on the dock today. Start familiarizing yourself with the [supply chain metrics](/posts/supply-chain-kpis-you-need-to-track) that matter to your leadership team and build a habit of tracking them weekly.

The transition is not easy, and most new managers struggle for the first few months. That is normal. The ones who succeed are the ones who recognize that the rules have changed and adapt their approach accordingly.
