## The Real Price of Inefficient Outbound Logistics

Most warehouse managers focus heavily on inbound operations, but outbound logistics often hides some of the most expensive inefficiencies in your facility. From missed delivery windows to poorly coordinated dock assignments, these hidden costs add up fast and eat into your margins.

### Key Takeaways

- **Detention fees compound quickly.** When outbound trucks sit idle waiting for loads, you are paying for that wasted time, either directly through carrier surcharges or indirectly through damaged relationships and higher future rates. Understanding [how detention and accessorial fees work](/posts/truck-detention-accessorial-fees) is the first step toward controlling them.

- **Dock scheduling eliminates guesswork.** Without a clear schedule for outbound shipments, your team ends up scrambling to prioritize loads, leading to overtime, errors, and late departures. A dock scheduling system gives your shipping team a clear picture of what is leaving and when.

- **Yard visibility matters for outbound too.** Knowing exactly which trailers are loaded, staged, and ready to go prevents the last-minute scramble that causes delays. When your [yard management process](/posts/yard-management-process-flow) is dialed in, outbound trucks move faster and more predictably.

If your outbound logistics costs keep climbing and you cannot pinpoint why, the answer is usually buried in the small inefficiencies that happen between the warehouse floor and the gate. Start tracking those blind spots and you will see where the money is going.
