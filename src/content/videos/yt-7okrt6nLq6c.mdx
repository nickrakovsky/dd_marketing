## Fixing Broken Warehouse Processes From the Dock to the Floor

If your warehouse feels like it is constantly putting out fires, the problem usually is not your people. It is your processes. Broken workflows at the dock, in the yard, and on the floor create a chain reaction of delays, errors, and unnecessary costs that most teams have simply learned to live with.

### Key Takeaways

- **Start at the dock door.** Most warehouse inefficiencies begin the moment a truck arrives. If there is no clear appointment schedule, no defined unloading priority, and no communication between the yard and the floor, everything downstream suffers. Getting your [dock scheduling process](/posts/what-is-dock-scheduling) right is the foundation for everything else.

- **Standardize before you automate.** Jumping straight to technology without fixing the underlying process just means you automate bad habits. Map out your current workflows, identify where delays and errors happen most, and standardize those steps first. Then layer in tools like a dock scheduling or [yard management system](/posts/what-is-a-yard-management-system-yms) to enforce consistency.

- **Measure what matters.** You cannot fix what you do not track. Start capturing basic metrics like dock-to-stock time, average truck turnaround, and appointment adherence. These numbers tell you exactly where your processes are breaking down and give you the data you need to justify improvements. Check out our guide on [warehouse optimization strategies](/posts/warehouse-optimization-strategies) for more on this.

Broken processes are not permanent. They just need someone willing to step back, look at the full picture, and make changes one step at a time.
